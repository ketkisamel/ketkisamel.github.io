About me

I'm a UC Berkeley senior studying integrative biology with a particular passion for birds, including behavior, evolution and conservation. I have field experience working on rodents in the Sierra Nevada, but I'm always looking for new opportunities to learn more about the natural world in a hands-on setting. I'd love to work in an environment where I can also put my skills in wildlife photography, writing and science education to good use!

Research

As an assistant to the UC Berkeley doctoral candidate Kwasi Wrensford, I study behavioral responses to environmental change in two chipmunk species, Tamias alpinus and Tamias speciosus. After receiving training on live-animal research from the UC Berkeley Office for Animal Care and Use, I handled live animals, collected tissue samples and measurements, and conducted behavioral trials during a monthlong trip to the eastern Sierra Nevada (Inyo and Mono Counties). After returning home, I analyzed videos of behavioral data using Behavioral Observation Research Interactive Software (BORIS). I am now manipulating and interpreting our data using R.
